The target started as a low-noise web property, but even a calm surface becomes
interesting once you shape the workflow around quick validation.

I started with <ToolPill tool="nmap" /> to confirm exposed services and capture
a baseline before touching the HTTP layer. From there, <ToolPill tool="ffuf" />
was enough to pull hidden directories into view while keeping the wordlist tight.

Once the initial routes were mapped, <ToolPill tool="burp" label="Burp Suite" />
gave me the fast feedback loop I needed for session replay and parameter review.

## Why the sequence mattered

1. Port and service discovery reduced guesswork.
2. Content discovery narrowed the routes worth testing.
3. Manual validation in the proxy separated real findings from noisy assumptions.

## Working notes

```bash
nmap -sV -Pn target.example
ffuf -u https://target.example/FUZZ -w ./wordlists/common.txt
```
